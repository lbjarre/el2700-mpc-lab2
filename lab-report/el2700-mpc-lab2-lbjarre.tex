\section{Vehicle model}
The first step of designing a good MPC is to obtain a good model of the system which is to be controlled.

\subsection{Continous time system}
The bicycle model in continous time
\begin{equation}
  \dot{\begin{bmatrix}
    x(t) \\
    y(t) \\
    v(t) \\
    \psi(t) \\
  \end{bmatrix}} = \begin{bmatrix}
    v(t)\cos\left(\psi(t) + \beta(t)\right) \\
    v(t)\sin\left(\psi(t) + \beta(t)\right) \\
    a(t) \\
    \frac{v(t)}{l_r}\sin\left(\beta(t)\right) \\
  \end{bmatrix}
\end{equation}

\subsection{Discrete time system}
In order to implement the MPC the model needs to be discretesized. This is here done by the explicit Euler method, which yields the system:
\begin{equation}
  \mathbf{z}(k+1) = \mathbf{z}(k) + T_sf(\mathbf{z}, \mathbf{u})
\end{equation}
